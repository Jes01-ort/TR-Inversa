# Entry of U.S. Military Personnel into Mexico

## Document 1 – Letter

[SEAL: UNITED MEXICAN STATES – PRESIDENCY OF THE REPUBLIC]

JOSÉ GERARDO RODOLFO FERNÁNDEZ NOROÑA<br> 
PRESIDENT OF THE BOARD OF DIRECTORS<br>
OF THE SENATE OF THE<br>
HONORABLE CONGRESS OF THE UNION<br>

Article 76, Section III, of the Political Constitution of the United Mexican States provides that it is the exclusive authority of the Senate of the Republic to authorize the Federal Executive to allow the transit of foreign troops through the national territory.

The Secretariat of the Navy received the invitation from the Defense Coordination Office of the Embassy of the United States of America to carry out the training event entitled **"Strengthening the Capacity of the SEMAR Special Operations Forces"**, which, if approved, will take place at the Specialized Training and Instruction Center for Marine Infantry located in San Luis Carpizo, Campeche, from February 17 to March 28, 2025.

The purpose of this event is for members of the Special Operations Forces Command of the United States Army and personnel of the Special Operations Unit of the Secretariat of the Navy to carry out training activities related to the execution of conventional and unconventional combat tactics, techniques, and procedures in amphibious operations in maritime, land, and air environments.

Should authorization be granted by that legislative body, a delegation composed of ten (10) members of the Seventh Special Forces Group of the United States of America would enter the national territory, carrying the weaponry, ammunition, materiel, and special equipment necessary for the joint training.

The instructors will arrive at Campeche International Airport aboard a C-130 military aircraft belonging to the United States Army on February 15, 2025, and will depart the country on March 30, 2025.

Therefore, pursuant to Article 76, Section III, of the Political Constitution of the United Mexican States, I submit for consideration by that Senate of the Honorable Congress of the Union the request for authorization to permit the entry of United States Army military personnel into the national territory, in order to participate in the training activity entitled “Strengthening the Capacity of SEMAR Special Operations Forces,” to be held at the Specialized Training and Instruction Center for Marine Infantry located in San Luis Carpizo, Campeche, from February 17 to March 28, 2025.

## Document 2 – Decree

### Official Gazette of the Federation

Executive Branch / PRESIDENCY OF THE REPUBLIC

**Published:** 02-17-2025

**Section:** Single

**Morning Edition** 

**Published:** 02-17-2025

**DECREE granting authorization to the President of the United Mexican States to allow the entry into national territory of ten (10) military personnel of the United States Army, in order to participate in the training activity entitled “Strengthening the Capacity of SEMAR Special Operations Forces,” to be held at the Specialized Training and Instruction Center for Marine Infantry located in San Luis Carpizo, Campeche, from February 17 to March 28, 2025.**

**In the margin, a seal bearing the National Coat of Arms that reads: United Mexican States – Presidency of the Republic.**

**CLAUDIA SHEINBAUM PARDO**, President of the United Mexican States, to its inhabitants, be it known:

That the Senate of the Honorable Congress of the Union has seen fit to address to me the following

**DECREE**

"THE SENATE OF THE HONORABLE CONGRESS OF THE UNION, IN EXERCISE OF THE AUTHORITY CONFERRED UPON IT BY ARTICLE 76, SECTION III OF THE POLITICAL CONSTITUTION OF THE UNITED MEXICAN STATES,

**DECREES:**

**First.**- The Senate of the Honorable Congress of the Union, in exercise of the authority granted to it by Article 76, Section III of the Political Constitution of the United Mexican States, grants authorization to the President of the United Mexican States to allow the entry into national territory of ten (10) military personnel of the United States Army, in order to participate in the training activity entitled "**Strengthening the Capacity of SEMAR Special Operations Forces**", to be held at the Specialized Training and Instruction Center for Marine Infantry located in San Luis Carpizo, Campeche, from February 17 to March 28, 2025.

**Second.**- The Head of the Federal Executive is respectfully requested to instruct the Secretary of the Navy to submit to this Sovereign Body a report on the results of the training exercise.

**Transitory Provisions**

**First.**- This Decree shall enter into force upon its approval by the Senate.

**Second.**- Publish in the Official Gazette of the Federation.

Mexico City, February 11, 2025.- Sen. Gerardo Fernández Noroña, President.- Sen. Verónica Noemí Camino Farjat, Secretary.- Signatures."

In compliance with the provisions of Section I of Article 89 of the Political Constitution of the United Mexican States, and for its due publication and observance, I issue this Decree at the Residence of the Federal Executive in Mexico City, on February 13, 2025.- **Claudia Sheinbaum Pardo**, President of the United Mexican States.- Signature.- **Rosa Icela Rodríguez Velázquez**, Secretary of the Interior.- Signature.

With a unanimous vote, President Claudia Sheinbaum Pardo obtained *authorization from the Senate plenary to allow the entry of 10 members of the United States Army into Mexican territory*, in order to participate in the training activities entitled “Strengthening the Capacity of SEMAR Special Operations Forces.”

This training will take place from *February 17 to March 28 of this year* at the Specialized Training and Instruction Center for Marine Infantry located in San Luis Carpizo, Campeche.

Senator Lilly Téllez of the PAN stated on the floor that this type of international activity “reminds us how cooperation between both military forces, the United States and Mexican forces, is possible.”

“As we can see, there is no invasion of Mexico because United States forces come to cooperate with Mexican forces; there is no invasion, no violation of sovereignty. The country was not sold, nor was it subordinated, because this cooperation between United States and Mexican forces occurs constantly.

“Why, then, not accept cooperation with the United States to act against the cartels in Mexico under a cooperation agreement within a legal framework, as is happening and has happened over the years when these types of exercises are carried out?” she questioned.

She stated that not only **would there be no violation of our sovereignty with collaboration with the United States** against the cartels, but that the lives of Mexicans could be saved, “whom Morena has left defenseless in the face of organized crime’s war against citizens. Thus, we see that it is clear that cooperation can occur without subordination,” she insisted.

#### The Mexican State cannot confront the cartels

Lilly Téllez stated that the reality is that the Mexican State cannot confront the cartels, because otherwise it would have already acted.

When presenting the draft decree, Senator Rolando Zapata Bello, secretary of the Navy Commission, stated that collaboration with the United States to train Mexican Navy personnel represents a significant opportunity to strengthen national security.

He stated that the draft decree has been carefully analyzed to **ensure that it preserves our sovereignty and strengthens our national capabilities**.

"Joint training with the United States Armed Forces can strengthen our combat, rescue, navigation, and tactical equipment handling capabilities, promoting professionalism and efficiency of our Navy to better respond to the growing threats facing the country,” argued the former governor of Yucatán.

## Document 4 – News Article 2

### El Heraldo de Saltillo | MEXICO

### Entry of United States military personnel for training of Navy members now official

By Agencies – February 17, 2025

![Personal militar resguarda una zona con vehículos oficiales mientras se forma en línea durante un operativo de seguridad](https://raw.githubusercontent.com/alainamb/uic_tr18-trad-inversa-es-en/main/unidad4/semana11/imagenes/Agencias_noticia2-image.png)

<small>Navy joins Easter security operation</small>

**Mexico City.-** On February 17, the decree authorizing the entry of United States military personnel into Mexico to train Special Operations personnel of the Secretariat of the Navy was published in the Official Gazette of the Federation (DOF).

Their presence in Mexico aims to participate in the training exercise entitled “Strengthening the Capacity of SEMAR Special Operations Forces,” to be held at the Specialized Training and Instruction Center for Marine Infantry located in San Luis Carpizo, Campeche, from February 17 to March 28, 2025.

The decree was approved by the Senate in exercise of the authority granted by Article 76, Section III of the Political Constitution of the United Mexican States and subsequently promulgated by the President of the United Mexican States, Claudia Sheinbaum Pardo.

The document also instructs the Secretariat of the Navy to submit a report to the Senate on the results of the exercise once the training is completed.

This authorization falls within the procedures established in the Constitution and current legislation, which require Senate approval to allow the temporary presence of foreign troops in the country. The training activity responds to cooperation agreements on security and defense between Mexico and the United States.

The authorization occurs at a time when bilateral security relations are marked by surveillance flights near Mexican territory, as well as the process in Washington to designate several Mexican cartels as foreign terrorist organizations (FTO).

The United States Department of State is advancing the process of designating several Mexican cartels as foreign terrorist organizations, which would strengthen sanctions against these groups and expand the operational capacity of United States security agencies.

The newspaper The New York Times reported that the list would include six Mexican cartels:

- Sinaloa Cartel (CDS)
- Jalisco New Generation Cartel (CJNG)
- Northeast Cartel
- La Familia Michoacana
- United Cartels
- Gulf Cartel (CDG)

This measure is based on an executive order signed on January 20, which seeks to classify these groups under the same category as Al-Qaeda or the Islamic State (ISIS). This designation would allow the United States to freeze assets, restrict financing, and expand jurisdiction in combating transnational organized crime. (EL HERALDO)
